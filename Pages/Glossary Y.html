Yoga :-

A system of personal development involving an integrated approach of mind and body control to promote health and inner peace. It usually involves gentle activities designed to maintain fitness, suppleness and muscle tone as well as to strengthen the body’s own healing powers. Breathing is important and together with meditation and visualization exercises, is thought to help energize body and mind, reduce stress, and improve concentration and clarity.

Young-onset PD :-

Medically, ‘young on-set Parkinson’s disease’ describes someone who develops the symptoms of PD after the age of 21 years and before the age of 40. In other contexts the term may be used to mean people of working age (i.e. under 65). The phrase ‘early-onset Parkinson’s disease’ is also sometimes used. Although there is no difference in the treatments available to treat younger and older people with PD, management of PD in younger people has to take into account the fact that they will live with PD for much longer than most older people, their response to the medication can be much more sensitive and they are at a different stage in their lives - e.g. still earning a living, raising children, paying mortgages.
