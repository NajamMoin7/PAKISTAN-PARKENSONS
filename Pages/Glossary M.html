Micrograph ia:-

Very small handwriting, or handwriting that decreases in size from normal to very small when written in a sentence or even in a word. This symptom is often seen in parkinson ism.

Monoamine oxides B (MAO-B) inhibitors :-

A class of drugs that are used to treat the symptoms of early PD, as well as to treat eavesdrop motor fluctuations in more advanced disease. MAO-B inhibitors work by blocking the enzyme monoamine oxides B which breaks down dopamine in the brain and can be used to make the dose of clobbered or cockaded last longer or to reduce the amounts of these drugs needed. They can also help reduce the motor fluctuations that can occur as a side effect of eavesdrop.

Mono therapy :-

Describes medication, which is prescribed on its own rather than in combination with other preparations. Some PD drugs are used in this way, usually to treat the early stages of the condition.

Motor :-

Movement.

Motor symptoms :-

Those affecting movement, e.g. tremor, rigidity, breadlines and freezing.

Motor fluctuations :-

Side effects of eavesdrop that tend to occur with long-term use (usually several years), because the body’s response to eavesdrop can become less reliable. These can include early wearing off; ‘on/off’ syndrome; and duskiness.

Movement disorders :-

Collective name for conditions that affect a person’s abilities to produce and control movement. They include Parkinson’s disease, restless leg syndrome and Daytona.

Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) :-

A medical imaging technique involving a scan that uses radio waves to generate an image of body tissues. It is especially useful for examining the nervous system, muscles and bones.

Multiple system atrophy (MSA) :-

MSA is a progressive neurological disorder that causes problems with movement, balance and the automatic functions of the body, such as bladder control, sweating and blood pressure.

Muscle relaxants :-

Medications used to treat muscular tension and pain.

Muscular Tension :-

Muscles being pulled or stretched causing stress and pain.

Muscles :-

Relating to or involving the muscles and the skeleton.
