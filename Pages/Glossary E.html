Essential tremor (ET) :-

ET is a common neurological condition, often misdiagnosed as Parkinson’s. It’s cause is unknown, but can run in families, resulting in fast, rhythmic trembling in the hands, head, legs, trunk or voice. Whilst the tremors experienced by people with Parkinson’s are apparent during rest periods, ET is an ‘action’ tremor - it is more pronounced when the affected part of the body is being used, for example in the hands when writing.

Extension :-

In deep brain stimulation, an insulated wire placed under the scalp that connects to the lead and runs behind the ear, down the neck and into the chest below the collar-bone where it connects to the neurosis.

Extra pyramidal system :-

Part of the nervous system that extends from the cortex to the medulla in the brain, from which emerge descending spinal pathways that influence voluntary motor activity throughout the body.
